\section{Scope}

This document records the mathematical model implemented by
\texttt{ekf/src/align.rs}. The align filter is a standalone IMU-to-vehicle
mount estimator. It is not the augmented ESKF; it only estimates the mount
quaternion and its three-dimensional small-angle covariance. The runtime fusion
pipeline uses this mount as the coarse seed for the ESKF and loose-reference
paths.

\section{Frames and Quaternion Convention}

\begin{longtable}{>{\raggedright\arraybackslash}p{0.12\linewidth} >{\raggedright\arraybackslash}p{0.78\linewidth}}
\toprule
\textbf{Symbol} & \textbf{Meaning} \\
\midrule
\(n\) & Local navigation frame, NED: north, east, down. GNSS velocities supplied to align are in this frame. \\
\(b\) & Raw IMU body frame. Mean gyroscope and accelerometer samples are expressed in this frame. \\
\(v\) & Vehicle frame, FRD: forward, right, down. The non-holonomic assumption constrains lateral and vertical vehicle velocity. \\
\bottomrule
\end{longtable}

The project math notes use active rotation matrices
\begin{equation}
  x_a = C_{ab} x_b ,
\end{equation}
where \(C_{ab}\) maps a vector from frame \(b\) into frame \(a\). Quaternions
use the same direction: \(q_{ab}\) denotes \(C_{ab}\), and
\begin{equation}
  C(q_1 \otimes q_2)=C(q_1)C(q_2).
\end{equation}

The align state stores \texttt{q\_vb}. In code this quaternion is converted to a
matrix denoted here as \(C_{bv}\), the vehicle-to-body mount rotation:
\begin{equation}
  x_b = C_{bv}(q_{vb}) x_v .
\end{equation}
Its transpose rotates measured IMU vectors into the vehicle frame:
\begin{equation}
  x_v = C_{vb} x_b = C_{bv}\T x_b .
\end{equation}

For small-angle corrections, the implementation uses
\begin{equation}
  \dq(\delta\theta) \simeq
  \begin{bmatrix}1 & \frac{1}{2}\delta\theta\T\end{bmatrix}\T .
\end{equation}
General roll/pitch/yaw mount corrections are left-injected,
\begin{equation}
  q_{vb}^{+}=\dq(\delta\theta)\otimes q_{vb}^{-},
\end{equation}
while the horizontal heading update applies a vehicle-frame yaw correction on
the right,
\begin{equation}
  q_{vb}^{+}=q_{vb}^{-}\otimes q_z(\delta\psi_v).
\end{equation}

\section{State and Covariance}

The nominal state is only the mount quaternion:
\begin{equation}
  x = q_{vb}\in \mathbb{S}^{3}.
\end{equation}
The covariance is defined on a three-state small-angle error:
\begin{equation}
  \delta x =
  \begin{bmatrix}
  \delta\phi & \delta\theta & \delta\psi
  \end{bmatrix}\T \in \R^3,
  \qquad
  P\in\R^{3\times 3}.
\end{equation}
The state order is roll, pitch, yaw of the mount error. Standard-deviation
configuration fields are one-sigma values; the filter stores variances in \(P\).

\section{Stationary Initialization}

Given stationary accelerometer samples \(f_{b,k}\), the bootstrap mean is
\begin{equation}
  \bar f_b=\frac{1}{N}\sum_{k=1}^{N}f_{b,k}.
\end{equation}
At rest, the measured specific force is approximately opposite vehicle down, so
the vehicle down axis expressed in body coordinates is initialized as
\begin{equation}
  \hat z_v^b = -\frac{\bar f_b}{\|\bar f_b\|}.
\end{equation}
The forward axis is chosen by projecting a reference body \(x\)-axis into the
plane orthogonal to \(\hat z_v^b\):
\begin{equation}
  \tilde x_v^b = x_{\mathrm{ref}} -
  \hat z_v^b(\hat z_v^b)\T x_{\mathrm{ref}},
  \qquad
  \hat x_v^b=\frac{\tilde x_v^b}{\|\tilde x_v^b\|}.
\end{equation}
If the projection degenerates, the code switches to a body \(y\)-axis reference.
The lateral axis is
\begin{equation}
  \hat y_v^b =
  \frac{\hat z_v^b \times \hat x_v^b}
       {\|\hat z_v^b \times \hat x_v^b\|},
  \qquad
  \hat x_v^b \leftarrow
  \frac{\hat y_v^b \times \hat z_v^b}
       {\|\hat y_v^b \times \hat z_v^b\|}.
\end{equation}
The tilt-only mount matrix uses these axes as body-frame columns:
\begin{equation}
  C_{bv}^{\mathrm{tilt}} =
  \begin{bmatrix}
  \hat x_v^b & \hat y_v^b & \hat z_v^b
  \end{bmatrix}.
\end{equation}
A supplied yaw seed is then applied as a vehicle-frame yaw delta:
\begin{equation}
  C_{bv,0}=C_{bv}^{\mathrm{tilt}} C_z(\psi_{\mathrm{seed}}-\psi_{\mathrm{tilt}}).
\end{equation}
After initialization the covariance is reset to tight roll/pitch uncertainty
and wide yaw uncertainty.

\section{Prediction}

Align prediction does not mechanize attitude with gyro integration. The nominal
mount is constant:
\begin{equation}
  q_{vb,k+1}^{-}=q_{vb,k}^{+}.
\end{equation}
Only the diagonal covariance receives random-walk process noise:
\begin{equation}
  P_{ii,k+1}^{-}=P_{ii,k}^{+}+\sigma_{q,i}^{2}\Delta t,
  \qquad i\in\{\phi,\theta,\psi\}.
\end{equation}

\section{Shared Observation Linearization}

For a window mean gyroscope \(\omega_b\) and a body-frame acceleration-like
vector \(a_b\), the code predicts vehicle-frame channels as
\begin{equation}
  h(q_{vb}) =
  \begin{bmatrix}
  \omega_v \\ a_v
  \end{bmatrix}
  =
  \begin{bmatrix}
  C_{vb}\omega_b \\ C_{vb}a_b
  \end{bmatrix},
  \qquad C_{vb}=C_{bv}\T .
\end{equation}
With the implemented left small-angle perturbation of \(q_{vb}\), the generated
Jacobian rows used by the Kalman updates are
\begin{equation}
  H_\omega = C_{vb}\crossmat{\omega_b},
  \qquad
  H_a = C_{vb}\crossmat{a_b}.
\end{equation}
Masked rows zero selected columns when an observation should not update yaw or
when yaw receives an extra scalar scale.

Scalar and two-row updates use standard linear Kalman equations:
\begin{align}
  r &= z-h(\hat x),\\
  S &= HPH\T+R,\\
  K &= PH\T S^{-1},\\
  \delta x &= Kr,\\
  P^{+} &\leftarrow (I-KH)P^{-}.
\end{align}
After each update, the small angle is injected into \(q_{vb}\) and \(P\) is
symmetrized. The implementation uses the simplified covariance update above,
not the Joseph form.

\section{Stationary Gravity Observation}

Stationary windows are detected by low gyro norm, accelerometer norm near
standard gravity, and near-zero GNSS speed. During such windows, the low-pass
gravity vector is updated:
\begin{equation}
  g_{b,\mathrm{lp}}^{+}
  =(1-\alpha)g_{b,\mathrm{lp}}^{-}+\alpha\bar f_b .
\end{equation}
The gravity observation rotates this vector into the vehicle frame:
\begin{equation}
  g_v=C_{vb}g_{b,\mathrm{lp}} .
\end{equation}
For a level stationary vehicle, the constrained channels are
\begin{equation}
  g_{v,y}\approx 0,\qquad g_{v,z}\approx -\|g_{b,\mathrm{lp}}\|.
\end{equation}
The current implementation applies the \(y\) and \(z\) rows with a roll/pitch
mask, so yaw is not directly updated by stationary gravity.

\section{GNSS-Derived Horizontal Heading Observation}

For consecutive GNSS velocities \(v_{n,k-1}\) and \(v_{n,k}\),
\begin{equation}
  a_n=\frac{v_{n,k}-v_{n,k-1}}{\Delta t}.
\end{equation}
Using the midpoint horizontal velocity direction
\begin{equation}
  \hat t=\frac{[v_N,v_E]\T}{\sqrt{v_N^2+v_E^2}},
  \qquad
  \hat \ell=\begin{bmatrix}-\hat t_E & \hat t_N\end{bmatrix}\T ,
\end{equation}
the GNSS longitudinal and lateral accelerations are
\begin{equation}
  a_{\mathrm{long}}=\hat t\T a_{n,NE},
  \qquad
  a_{\mathrm{lat}}=\hat\ell\T a_{n,NE}.
\end{equation}

The body accelerometer vector is first stripped of its gravity-axis component.
Using the current mount, the body gravity axis is
\begin{equation}
  \hat g_b=-C_{bv}e_z,
\end{equation}
and the horizontal body acceleration is
\begin{equation}
  a_{b,h}=\bar f_b-\hat g_b\hat g_b\T\bar f_b .
\end{equation}
It is then expressed in the vehicle frame:
\begin{equation}
  a_{v,h}=C_{vb}a_{b,h}.
\end{equation}

When speed and acceleration gates pass, align compares the two horizontal
directions by
\begin{equation}
  e_\psi =
  \operatorname{atan2}
  \left(
    a_{v,h,x}a_{\mathrm{lat}} - a_{v,h,y}a_{\mathrm{long}},
    a_{v,h,x}a_{\mathrm{long}} + a_{v,h,y}a_{\mathrm{lat}}
  \right).
\end{equation}
The residual is treated as a vehicle-frame yaw observation:
\begin{equation}
  \delta\psi_v=-K_\psi e_\psi,\qquad
  K_\psi=\frac{P_{\psi\psi}}{P_{\psi\psi}+R_\psi}.
\end{equation}
The yaw correction is right-injected as \(q_{vb}^{+}=q_{vb}^{-}\otimes
q_z(\delta\psi_v)\). Roll/yaw and pitch/yaw cross-covariances are zeroed after
this scalar yaw update.

\section{Turn Consistency and Planar-Gyro Observation}

Turn windows require minimum speed, course rate, and lateral acceleration.
Before turn-heading observations receive full trust, a ring buffer checks
\begin{equation}
  a_{\mathrm{lat}}\approx v\,\dot\chi
\end{equation}
for sign and magnitude consistency over several retained windows.

When the turn gyro path is enabled, the body gyro mean is rotated into vehicle
coordinates:
\begin{equation}
  \omega_v=C_{vb}\bar\omega_b .
\end{equation}
The planar-motion constraint is
\begin{equation}
  \omega_{v,x}\approx 0,\qquad \omega_{v,y}\approx 0.
\end{equation}
By default this update affects roll and pitch. It may update yaw only if the
configured turn-gyro yaw scale is positive and turn-heading consistency has
passed.

\section{Readiness and Known Limitations}

The align filter reports coarse alignment ready after yaw has been observed and
the one-sigma roll, pitch, and yaw covariances are all below the configured
ready gates. Important limitations of the current model are:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em]
\item Gravity observes roll and pitch only during stationary windows.
\item Horizontal acceleration is reduced to a heading/yaw residual; it does not
directly solve a full vector mount least-squares problem.
\item The mount process model is a random walk with no vehicle dynamics.
\item The yaw update is a scalar vehicle-frame correction, while other mount
updates use left-injected body-frame small angles.
\end{itemize}
